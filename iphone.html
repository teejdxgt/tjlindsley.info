iPhone Applications

In my free time I also enjoy building iPhone applications in objective-c from time to time

I've gone through multiple versions of the Stanford iOS development classes to build my skills and create apps to solve problems for me.

36 Hour Destinations

This is the most recent iPhone application that I have built. It uses the New York Times Articles API to aggregate all of their 36 Hours in a Destination stories into a single location (grouped by country).

The app is pretty basic, but I have many ideas for future enhancements. Right now it paginates through the New York Times Article API to find all of the 36 Hours stories and saves their meta data onto the phone (for quick load times). The articles are then grouped by country, searchable by city, and can be saved as favorites and for offline use (in the event of no cell coverage).

This app has been submitted to the app store and is currently in queue to be reviewed by Apple legal team to ensure that my use of the NY Times data does not violate any terms of service...fingers crossed.

Marta Train GPS

Before Atlanta's Marta train data was available via their API I had a friend who worked for Sign Posts Networks and made the real train time (synced to the station electronic signs) data available via a simple html page.

I built an iPhone application that screen scraped his page and displayed the real time train arrival times in a UITableView. The stations we searchable and there was also a map that could be zoomed in on.

In the end, my friend stopped working for sign post networks and my data source stopped working, so I removed the app from the app store. I've though about updating my app to work with the Marta API, but the One Bus Away app is so good already there is really no need for Marta Train GPS anymore.
