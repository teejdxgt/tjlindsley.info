In 2013 my fianc√© and I were planning our wedding and I felt overwhelmed by all of the disparate items that needed to be tracked, communicated, and coordinated.

To preserve our sanity, I used knowledge from Vitrue to build a Rails application that would solve most of our problems for us.

www.scottieandtj.com

While it didn't have the most modern design ever, scottieandtj.com was incredibly helpful in organizing our wedding and also gave me a much better understanding of Rails in general.

The application provided the usual wedding information that guests need like times, places, hotels, registries, etc (using a style sheet I was very proud of at the time), and also pulled in Facebook info to form a guest list, allowed messages to be posted (but only view if guests were following each other -- like twitter), and also tracked RSVPs from the site.

In the end the site proved to be both fun to build and extremely useful. Paper RSVPs and mass emails were never necessary for the wedding thanks it's functionality.
